What Goes In

Five Ingredients.
No Compromises.

01 — Milk

Reduced for Hours, Not Minutes

Full-cream milk is simmered slowly over an open flame until it thickens into khoya — the base of nearly everything we make.

02 — Desi Ghee

Clarified the Traditional Way

Our ghee is hand-churned from cultured cream and slow-cooked until golden — the difference you taste in every bite.

03 — Cardamom

Cracked Just Before Use

Green cardamom pods are cracked and ground fresh each morning, so the fragrance never fades before it reaches you.

04 — Saffron

The Finest Kashmiri Threads

Only hand-picked Grade A1 saffron is used — enough to colour and perfume, never so much that it overwhelms.

05 — Pistachio

Sliced by Hand, Sheet by Sheet

Every pistachio is sliced by hand for garnish — a slow, deliberate finish machines simply cannot replicate.

The Process

From Milk to Doorstep.

1

Milk

Full-cream milk arrives before sunrise from trusted local dairies within a day's reach of our kitchen.

2

Cooking

It is simmered for hours in wide copper vessels, reduced slowly until it thickens into khoya.

3

Roasting

Nuts and gram flour are roasted in pure desi ghee until the aroma fills the entire kitchen.

4

Mixing

Saffron, cardamom, and khoya are folded together by hand, in ratios our karigars know by memory.

5

Hand Rolling

Each ladoo, barfi, and peda is shaped individually — no two pieces are ever machine-identical.

6

Decoration

Silver vark, pistachio slivers, and saffron strands are placed by hand, piece by piece.

7

Packaging

Sweets rest in breathable boxes lined with butter paper, sealed with a wax-stamped ribbon.

8

Delivery

Boxed the same day they're made, and on your table the same day they're ordered in-city.

Our Story

Started in a
Home Kitchen.

In 1978, Nandini Devi began making mithai for neighbours in Jaipur — small batches, made with ghee her own mother taught her to clarify. There was no shop, only word of mouth.

Nearly five decades later, the recipes haven't changed, even if the kitchen has grown. Every sweet leaving our workshop today is still checked, by hand, against the standard she set at her own stove.

— The Nandini Family
